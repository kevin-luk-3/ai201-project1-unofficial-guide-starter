=== MOVIE: The Fast and the Furious ===
YEAR: 2001
SOURCE: Wikipedia
SOURCE_URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Fast_and_the_Furious

## Plot
LAPD officer Brian O'Conner goes undercover in LA street-racing culture to bust truck hijacker Dominic Toretto. Brian bonds with Dom and his crew while investigating; he ultimately lets Dom escape, choosing loyalty over the badge.

## Cast
Vin Diesel as Dominic Toretto, Paul Walker as Brian O'Conner, Michelle Rodriguez as Letty Ortiz, Jordana Brewster as Mia Toretto.

## Production
Directed by Rob Cohen. Inspired by a Vibe magazine article on illegal street racing. Shot in Los Angeles with practical car stunts.

## Release
Released June 22, 2001. Surprise summer hit that launched the franchise.

## Reception
Mixed reviews; praised for energy and car sequences, criticized for thin plot. Cult following grew on home video.

=== MOVIE: 2 Fast 2 Furious ===
YEAR: 2003
SOURCE: Wikipedia
SOURCE_URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2_Fast_2_Furious

## Plot
Brian O'Conner, now a fugitive, partners with childhood friend Roman Pearce in Miami to take down drug lord Carter Verone while working with undercover agent Monica Fuentes.

## Cast
Paul Walker, Tyrese Gibson as Roman Pearce, Eva Mendes, Ludacris as Tej Parker. Vin Diesel does not appear.

## Production
Directed by John Singleton. Franchise's only main entry without Dom Toretto.

## Release
Released June 6, 2003. Lower gross than the original.

## Reception
Generally negative reviews. Seen as a disconnected sequel lacking the Dom-Brian dynamic.

---

=== MOVIE: The Fast and the Furious: Tokyo Drift ===
YEAR: 2006
SOURCE: Wikipedia
SOURCE_URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Fast_and_the_Furious_Tokyo_Drift

## Plot
American teen Sean Boswell is sent to Tokyo and enters the underground drift-racing scene, clashing with yakuza-linked racer Takashi (DK) while mentored by Han Lue.

## Cast
Lucas Black as Sean Boswell, Sung Kang as Han Lue, Bow Wow, Brian Tee as DK. Vin Diesel cameo at end.

## Production
Justin Lin's franchise debut. First film set outside the US. Introduces Han, a fan-favorite character.

## Release
Released June 16, 2006. Softest box office of the series at the time.

## Reception
Mixed reviews; praised for Tokyo setting and drift scenes. Later reassessed as underrated by fans.

## Timeline
Release order: film 3. Internal chronology: set later in the franchise timeline (after F6 events in later retcons).

=== MOVIE: Fast & Furious ===
YEAR: 2009
SOURCE: Wikipedia
SOURCE_URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fast_&_Furious

## Plot
Dom returns to LA to avenge Letty's death and hunt drug trafficker Arturo Braga. Brian, now FBI, must work with Dom again. The team infiltrates Braga's operation across the US-Mexico border.

## Cast
Vin Diesel, Paul Walker, Michelle Rodriguez, Jordana Brewster reunited. Gal Gadot debuts as Gisele Yashar.

## Production
Justin Lin directs. Deliberate franchise soft reboot reuniting original cast.

## Release
Released April 3, 2009. Doubled prior entries' opening weekend.

## Reception
Modest critical reception; fans welcomed the cast reunion.

---

=== MOVIE: Fast Five ===
YEAR: 2011
SOURCE: Wikipedia
SOURCE_URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fast_Five

## Plot
Dom, Brian, and Mia flee to Rio de Janeiro and assemble a crew to steal $100 million from corrupt businessman Hernan Reyes while evading DSS agent Luke Hobbs.

## Cast
Vin Diesel, Paul Walker, Dwayne Johnson debuts as Luke Hobbs, Jordana Brewster, Ludacris, Tyrese Gibson, Sung Kang as Han, Gal Gadot.

## Production
Pivoted franchise from street racing to heist action. Vault chase filmed in Puerto Rico doubling for Rio.

## Release
Released April 29, 2011. Highest-grossing in series until Furious 7.

## Reception
Strongest critical reception of the franchise to that point. Often cited as the peak entry.

## Franchise Notes
Luke Hobbs first appears here. Crew-as-family template established.

---

=== MOVIE: Fast & Furious 6 ===
YEAR: 2013
SOURCE: Wikipedia
SOURCE_URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fast_&_Furious_6

## Plot
Hobbs recruits Dom's team to stop mercenary Owen Shaw in exchange for clearing their records. Letty returns alive but amnesiac. Han is killed in Tokyo-set sequence.

## Cast
Full ensemble returns. Gina Carano as Riley Hicks. Luke Evans as Owen Shaw.

## Production
Tank chase on Spanish highway among largest practical stunts in series.

## Release
Released May 24, 2013.

## Reception
Positive reviews for action; Han's death became emotional flashpoint for fans.

---

=== MOVIE: Furious 7 ===
YEAR: 2015
SOURCE: Wikipedia
SOURCE_URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Furious_7

## Plot
Dom's team hunts Deckard Shaw, who seeks revenge for his brother Owen. Mr. Nobody assists in exchange for help recovering God's Eye surveillance device.

## Cast
Paul Walker final completed role. Jason Statham as Deckard Shaw. Kurt Russell as Mr. Nobody.

## Production
Paul Walker died during filming; completed using brothers Caleb and Cody Walker and CGI.

## Release
Released April 3, 2015. Franchise box-office record.

## Reception
Emotional tribute ending widely praised. Strong critical and audience scores.

---

=== MOVIE: The Fate of the Furious ===
YEAR: 2017
SOURCE: Wikipedia
SOURCE_URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Fate_of_the_Furious

## Plot
Cipher coerces Dom into betraying his family by holding Elena and his son hostage. The team must stop her from stealing nuclear launch codes.

## Cast
Charlize Theron as Cipher. Helen Mirren debuts in franchise.

## Production
Submarine chase and zombie-car sequences push absurdity further.

## Release
Released April 14, 2017.

## Reception
Mixed reviews; spectacle praised, plot logic questioned.

---

=== MOVIE: F9: The Fast Saga ===
YEAR: 2021
SOURCE: Wikipedia
SOURCE_URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/F9_The_Fast_Saga

## Plot
Dom faces his estranged brother Jakob Toretto while the team hunts a device connected to their father's death. Flashbacks explain family rift.

## Cast
John Cena as Jakob Toretto. Han returns alive, explaining his survival.

## Production
Magnet-plane and rocket-car sequences. Justin Lin returns to direct.

## Release
Released June 25, 2021. Delayed by COVID-19.

## Reception
Polarized reception; space scene became meme. Han return celebrated by fans.

---

=== MOVIE: Fast X ===
YEAR: 2023
SOURCE: Wikipedia
SOURCE_URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fast_X

## Plot
Dante Reyes, son of Fast Five villain Hernan Reyes, targets Dom's family for revenge across Rome, Rio, and other locations. Ends on cliffhanger.

## Cast
Jason Momoa as Dante Reyes. Brie Larson joins as Tess.

## Production
Louis Leterrier replaced Justin Lin. Part one of planned two-part finale.

## Release
Released May 19, 2023.

## Reception
Momoa's villain praised; some criticism of overcrowded cast and cliffhanger.
