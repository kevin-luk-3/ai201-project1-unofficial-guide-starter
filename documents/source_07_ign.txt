=== The Fast and the Furious (2001) ===

REVIEW | Ign
Source: https://www.ign.com/movies/the-fast-and-the-furious
The film that launched a billion-dollar franchise. Thin plot but excellent chase scenes and undeniable chemistry between Diesel and Walker. It remembers summer movies from drive-in double features — slicker than Grand Theft Auto but the same pirate spirit. Pure early-2000s action comfort food.
Rob Cohen directs with a MTV-era visual snap that makes every race feel dangerous even when the plot is pure undercover-cop formula. Critics split on whether the film is empty calories or a perfect exploitation throwback, but the box-office success proved audiences wanted this specific blend of nitrous, found-family bonding, and Diesel's charisma. It set the template every later entry would either follow or deliberately break.

=== 2 Fast 2 Furious (2003) ===

REVIEW | Ign
Source: https://www.ign.com/movies/2-fast-2-furious
A hollow retread without Dominic Toretto. Flashy Miami races but the sequel lacks the emotional anchor of the original. Singleton keeps things moving but Paul Walker can't carry the film alone. Tyrese helps but doesn't save it.
John Singleton brings Miami color and Tyrese's comic relief, yet the absence of Dominic Toretto leaves a hole no amount of neon can fill. Critics compared it unfavorably to the first film's lean storytelling, and many ranked it among the franchise's weakest. The races look expensive but lack emotional stakes — a warning sign the series would need a reunion to recover.

=== The Fast and the Furious: Tokyo Drift (2006) ===

REVIEW | Ign
Source: https://www.ign.com/movies/the-fast-and-the-furious-tokyo-drift
A fresh start with new characters and genuine drifting spectacle. Weak lead performance but strong car action — the franchise's last honest racing movie for many fans. Han's introduction became one of the series' most beloved characters. Tokyo setting gives it a visual jolt.
Justin Lin's first entry in the series reframes the franchise around drift culture and a fish-out-of-water American in Tokyo. Lucas Black's lead performance is often criticized as wooden, but the mountain-road sequences and Han's cool detachment became touchstones for fans. Critics noted it was more observant about Japan than expected, even if the human drama never matched the tire smoke.

=== Fast & Furious (2009) ===

REVIEW | Ign
Source: https://www.ign.com/movies/fast-and-furious
Reuniting the original cast re-energized the series. More crime caper than street race, but the tunnel chase delivers. Smart about how far to stretch logic without sacrificing the visceral thrill. Bridge between street-racing roots and later heist direction.
The fourth film exists mainly to reunite Diesel and Walker and remind viewers why they cared about Dom and Brian in the first place. Critics praised the tunnel chase as a return to visceral stunt work while noting the crime plot was secondary to character nostalgia. Commercially it worked — the franchise was reborn as an action brand rather than a street-racing niche.

=== Fast Five (2011) ===

REVIEW | Ign
Source: https://www.ign.com/movies/fast-five
The franchise turning point — heist action, Rio setting, and Johnson vs Diesel make this the best entry for many fans. Relentlessly entertaining even when logic checks out. Vault chase in Rio is either iconic or ridiculous. What I missed were laughs, but the action delivers.
Justin Lin and Chris Morgan pivot the series into a Rio heist picture, adding Dwayne Johnson's Hobbs as both antagonist and franchise mascot. The vault-dragging set piece became the series' signature move toward physics-defying spectacle. Critics who liked it called it the best pure entertainment in the catalog; skeptics said it permanently abandoned racing for cartoon action — both camps still debate it.

=== Fast & Furious 6 (2013) ===

REVIEW | Ign
Source: https://www.ign.com/movies/fast-furious-6
Big dumb fun. The runway sequence goes on forever but fans eat it up. Hobbs and Shaw chemistry continues to work. Tank on the highway is peak Fast family energy. Less about racing, more about the crew as an action team.
The sixth entry leans into telenovela family drama and set pieces that would be impossible on any real runway. Letty's amnesia plot gives Dom emotional motivation beyond another MacGuffin, while the tank-on-the-highway sequence epitomizes the series' 'don't think, cheer' ethos. Critics acknowledged diminishing returns on logic but praised the cast chemistry that keeps the machine running.

=== Furious 7 (2015) ===

REVIEW | Ign
Source: https://www.ign.com/movies/furious-7
A blockbuster that earns its emotional beats. The Paul Walker farewell is handled with surprising grace amid the usual over-the-top set pieces. Abu Dhabi jump and parachuting cars are peak absurdity. You feel Walker's absence throughout.
James Wan brings horror-film pacing to blockbuster scale, and the Paul Walker farewell gives the chaos unexpected weight. The plane-drop sequence and Abu Dhabi tower jump are among the most audacious stunts in the series. Critics widely noted that the film balances grief and spectacle better than anyone expected from a seventh installment.

=== The Fate of the Furious (2017) ===

REVIEW | Ign
Source: https://www.ign.com/movies/the-fate-of-the-furious
Cipher is a standout villain even when the plot goes fully bonkers. New York zombie-car sequence is memorable. Dom going rogue divided fans. Fully in superhero territory — submarine chase included.
Charlize Theron's Cipher gives the franchise its most genuinely menacing villain since the first film's drug lord. Dom turned rogue by blackmail is a soap-opera twist that divided fans, while the submarine chase and zombie-car pile in New York pushed absurdity to new heights. Critics found plenty to mock in the dialogue but admitted the set pieces still deliver guilty-pleasure thrills.

=== F9: The Fast Saga (2021) ===

REVIEW | Ign
Source: https://www.ign.com/movies/f9
Underwhelming lead-up to the finale for some critics. Space magnet scene is where many fans checked out. Jason Momoa's Dante almost saves it for others. Physics completely gone — embrace the absurdity or bail.
Justin Lin returns to direct the most self-aware ridiculous entry yet, complete with magnet-powered space antics and a long-lost Toretto brother played by John Cena. Critics called it either joyful maximalism or proof the series had eaten itself. The film leans into family mythology so hard that even skeptics admit the stunt choreography and Lin's direction remain technically impressive.

=== Fast X (2023) ===

REVIEW | Ign
Source: https://sea.ign.com/fast-x/199213/review/fast-x-review
Jason Momoa's Dante deserves canonization as a top Fast villain. Not enough room at the table for Dom's ever-growing family. Flat tire of a part one but Momoa almost saves it. Convoluted twists play like a telenovela, but stunts are inspired.
Louis Leterrier steps in as director and Jason Momoa's Dante reframes the villain role as playful chaos rather than generic evil. The film juggles too many legacy characters for its own good and ends on a cliffhanger, frustrating completionists. Critics who stayed on board praised Momoa's scene-stealing energy and the Rome chase while acknowledging the plot is mostly setup for Part Two.
