=== The Fast and the Furious (2001) ===

REVIEW | Screenrant
Source: https://screenrant.com/fast-furious-movies-ranked/
The original remains a cult favorite for its grounded street-racing focus and the chemistry between Vin Diesel and Paul Walker — before the franchise went full superhero. It launched a billion-dollar saga from a simple undercover-cop vs street-racer plot. Many ranked lists still put Tokyo Drift and Fast Five above it, but none exist without this film.

---

=== 2 Fast 2 Furious (2003) ===

REVIEW | Screenrant
Source: https://screenrant.com/fast-furious-2-movie-2003-facts/
Without Vin Diesel, the sequel struggles. Paul Walker and Tyrese carry a Miami-set retread that fans often rank among the weakest entries. John Singleton directs flashy races but the story lacks the emotional anchor of the original. The rat torture scene is frequently cited as a tonal misfire.

---

=== The Fast and the Furious: Tokyo Drift (2006) ===

REVIEW | Screenrant
Source: https://screenrant.com/fast-furious-tokyo-drift-best-worst/
Tokyo Drift is the last mainline entry truly about street racing and car culture. Han and the drifting sequences became franchise icons even though it initially felt like a spinoff. Many lists call it underrated for car enthusiasts. The series later devolved into heist action and physics-defying stunts.

---

=== Fast & Furious (2009) ===

REVIEW | Screenrant
Source: https://screenrant.com/fast-furious-2009-reunion/
Bringing Dom and Brian back rebooted the series after Tokyo Drift. Fans were glad to see the original cast return even if the plot is a crime thriller with cars attached. The tunnel chase gets frequent praise. Continuity questions annoy some viewers but most agree the reunion worked commercially and creatively.

---

=== Fast Five (2011) ===

REVIEW | Screenrant
Source: https://screenrant.com/fast-five-best-franchise/
Many fans and critics call Fast Five the peak of the franchise. The Rio heist, the vault chase, and adding Dwayne Johnson turned street racers into an Ocean's Eleven-style action crew. It pivoted the series away from racing permanently. Johnson vs Diesel fight is a franchise highlight.

---

=== Fast & Furious 6 (2013) ===

REVIEW | Screenrant
Source: https://screenrant.com/fast-furious-6-tank-scene-highway/
The tank chase is one of the most talked-about sequences in the franchise — physics-defying but crowd-pleasing. Letty's return adds emotional weight for Dom. The runway finale at the end goes on forever but fans eat it up. Hobbs and Shaw continue to be a fan-favorite pairing.

---

=== Furious 7 (2015) ===

REVIEW | Screenrant
Source: https://screenrant.com/furious-7-paul-walker-tribute/
The ending tribute to Paul Walker had audiences in tears. Brian retires to be with family — a montage set to See You Again became one of the most emotional moments in blockbuster history. Cars parachuting from a plane is peak Fast absurdity. Critics praised how the film handled Walker's farewell.

---

=== The Fate of the Furious (2017) ===

REVIEW | Screenrant
Source: https://screenrant.com/fate-of-the-furious-submarine-chase/
The submarine sequence became a meme — peak Fast family nonsense. Fans debate if Dom-turned-villain works or if the series had fully jumped the shark. Cipher is widely praised as a villain. New York zombie-car sequence is another frequently cited set piece.

---

=== F9: The Fast Saga (2021) ===

REVIEW | Screenrant
Source: https://screenrant.com/f9-space-scene/
F9 works better if you accept it as fantasy, not racing. The magnet space scene is where many fans say the franchise crossed from ridiculous to self-parody. John Cena as Dom's brother divided audiences. Some loved the audacity; others say the series should have stopped earlier.

---

=== Fast X (2023) ===

REVIEW | Screenrant
Source: https://screenrant.com/fast-x-movie-dom-little-brian-scene-fan-reaction/
Jason Momoa's Dante carried the movie for many viewers. Dom's son leaping between speeding cars ignited debate about physics vs superhero aesthetics. Too many characters and a cliffhanger ending frustrated some fans. Momoa's playful villain energy was widely praised as the best part of Part One.
