=== The Fast and the Furious (2001) ===

REVIEW | Empire
Source: https://www.empireonline.com/movies/reviews/fast-furious-review/
A delightful oddity — a movie that does exactly what it says on the tin. The Fast and the Furious knows it's a B movie and delivers great chase scenes with style. Chemistry between Dom and Brian is the main highlight that started the whole franchise.
Empire praised the seamless blend of CGI and practical stunt work in the race sequences, calling it some of the finest auto-action on screen at the time. The review positioned the film as a knowing B-movie that never apologizes for its exploitation roots. Dom and Brian's dynamic is singled out as the emotional engine that would fuel a decade of sequels.

=== 2 Fast 2 Furious (2003) ===

REVIEW | Empire
Source: https://www.empireonline.com/movies/reviews/2-fast-2-furious-review/
Singleton keeps things moving but without Diesel the emotional core is missing. Flashy but forgettable compared to the original. Miami setting adds visual flair but the story can't match the first film.
Empire's take matched most critics: visually flashy but missing the grit and heart of the original. The Miami palette and Tyrese's energy add flavor, but the plot feels like a retread with worse stakes. The review is often cited in ranked lists as proof the franchise needed Diesel back.

---

=== The Fast and the Furious: Tokyo Drift (2006) ===

REVIEW | Empire
Source: https://www.empireonline.com/movies/reviews/fast-furious-tokyo-drift-review/
Unapologetic car porn. Best race action yet in the series at the time. Performances are stilted and the plot is forgettable — but that's not why we're here. Han and the drifting culture became franchise icons.
Empire called it unapologetic car porn with the best race action in the series to that point, even while panning the lead performance. Han's introduction and the drift sequences earned lasting praise in fan communities. The review captures why Tokyo Drift has been reassessed as underrated by car-culture audiences.

---

=== Fast & Furious (2009) ===

REVIEW | Empire
Source: https://www.empireonline.com/movies/reviews/fast-furious-4-review/
The reunion of Walker and Diesel gives this entry a jolt of energy. More focused on crime than racing but the tunnel sequence is a highlight. Bridge between street-racing roots and later heist direction.
Empire saw the reunion as a necessary course correction after Tokyo Drift's detour. The tunnel chase is highlighted as the set piece that justified the return to Dom and Brian. The review notes the shift from pure racing toward crime-caper structure that Fast Five would amplify.

---

=== Fast Five (2011) ===

REVIEW | Empire
Source: https://www.empireonline.com/movies/reviews/fast-five-review/
Dwayne Johnson hulks through leaving testosterone trails — the best thing in Fast Five. The heist pivot works better than it has any right to. Rio vault chase is either iconic or ridiculous depending on your tolerance.
Empire singled out Dwayne Johnson as the best new addition and praised the heist structure for refreshing a tired formula. The Rio vault sequence is described as either iconic or ridiculous depending on tolerance for physics violations. The review aligns with consensus that this is the franchise's creative peak.

---

=== Fast & Furious 6 (2013) ===

REVIEW | Empire
Source: https://www.empireonline.com/movies/reviews/fast-furious-6-review/
The tank scene is gloriously absurd. The franchise proves there's still life in the family. Runway finale goes on forever but fans eat it up. Less racing, more action team-up.
Empire celebrated the tank sequence as gloriously absurd and proof the family still has gas in the tank. The runway finale's length is noted with affectionate exasperation. The review treats the film as big dumb fun done with professional polish.

---

=== Furious 7 (2015) ===

REVIEW | Empire
Source: https://www.empireonline.com/movies/reviews/furious-7-review/
Just might be the best one yet in pure thrills. The Paul Walker farewell is handled with exceptional care amid the usual mayhem. Cars parachuting from a plane has to be seen to be believed.
Empire ranked it among the best for pure thrills and praised the handling of Paul Walker's farewell. The plane drop and emotional ending are both singled out as reasons the entry works beyond typical sequel fatigue. The review reflects widespread critical respect for how the film balanced tribute and spectacle.

---

=== The Fate of the Furious (2017) ===

REVIEW | Empire
Source: https://www.empireonline.com/movies/reviews/fate-furious-review/
The series goes full Bond villain with Charlize Theron. Ridiculous set pieces including a submarine chase. Dom going rogue divided fans but Cipher is widely praised.
Empire enjoyed Theron's Cipher and the Bond-villain escalation while acknowledging Dom-goes-evil as a divisive choice. The submarine chase is treated as meme-worthy peak Fast. The review captures critics enjoying the ride while rolling their eyes at the family sermonizing.

---

=== F9: The Fast Saga (2021) ===

REVIEW | Empire
Source: https://www.empireonline.com/movies/reviews/f9-review/
Bonkers even by Fast standards. The space magnet sequence divides fans like nothing before it. Long-lost brother plot with John Cena was hit or miss. Completionists still showed up.
Empire called it bonkers even by franchise standards and flagged the space sequence as the ultimate litmus test for fans. John Cena's brother plot and Lin's return are noted as either refreshing or repetitive depending on viewer patience. The review reflects middling critical scores with strong fan turnout.

---

=== Fast X (2023) ===

REVIEW | Empire
Source: https://www.empireonline.com/movies/reviews/fast-x-review/
Jason Momoa steals every scene as Dante. Overstuffed Part One that sets up bigger chaos to come. Cliffhanger ending frustrates but Momoa's energy carries it for many viewers.
Empire praised Jason Momoa as scene-stealing villain energy and criticized the overstuffed cast and Part One cliffhanger. The Rome set pieces and Dante's personal vendetta are seen as the film's strongest material. The review matches Metacritic consensus: fun set pieces, messy architecture.
